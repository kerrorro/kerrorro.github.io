A La Carte

KinomaJS

A La Carte is a companion mobile app for a simulated IoT smart shopping cart. Budget and health conscious shoppers are often faced with unending decision making upon entering grocery stores due to the sheer amount of products available. A La Carte seeks to provide a one-stop shopping solution by scanning the products placed in the shopping cart and showing key information such as running totals, calories per servings, and food group breakdowns. In addition, it speeds up the shopping experience by offering features such as quick check out and a product locator.

A La Carte was created for the UC Berkeley course User Interface Design and Development by me, Rohit Lalchandani, Lydia Gilbert, and Harry Li. Early prototypes were designed to test the original core features: a running total, calorie breakdowns, and product comparisons. After the first user test, we realized that the product comparison feature did not actually speed up the process of comparing two products and that our users would much rather prefer features that would help with making shopping faster. The final design opted for a product locator and quick check out.
For a more detailed look into the design and development process check out our Medium.
