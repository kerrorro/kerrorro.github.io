Knight Slayer

STENCYL // SCRUM FOR TRELLO

Knight Slayer is a 2D arcade-style hack and slash created as part of the Introduction to Game Development course taught at The University of Texas at Austin. The game included timed elements where the player would have to press the correct button corresponding to the incoming enemy before being struck. Players could earn power-ups for combos and also buy in-game content such as sprites with their kill counts.

Sam Kim (art/animation), Willie Man (programming), and myself (programming) created Knight Slayer using the Stencyl game engine. Development involved creating scenes, actors, and behaviors in a visual editor similar to MIT's Scratch. We used Trello to create user stories and assign tasks with estimated completion times for weekly sprints.

The game mechanics are as follows: A stationary knight fights incoming waves of enemies at increasing levels of difficulty (either faster walking or attacking speeds). As the enemies approach, a ground indicator will highlight indicating the correct hit timing. If the player hits too soon, the attack will miss and their stamina will decrease. Depleted stamina makes the knight unable to attack. Once the enemies reach the player, they attack continuously until defeated. Each enemy is also of a certain type. To defeat them, the player must remember the correct button to press.
